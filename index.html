Project Idea

Overview

This cleaning and chore scheduling app will assist users in organising a functional cleaning and chore checklist, with assorted tasks that need to be done with varying frequencies in all areas of the home. This checklist can also be viewed by other members of the household, and each person can have specific tasks or locations assigned to them throughout any given time period in order to help share the load.

Motivation

As someone who works full time and now studies part time, it is difficult to set up a cleaning structure at home. It’s very easy to just get home and do nothing. Something like this sending reminders out to do specific things every now and then could hopefully be the little push needed to help motivate one to perform cleaning duties throughout the week.

Description

This app will have numerous features in order to help you to keep your house clean.

While setting up this app, you will add in the number of rooms and separate areas in your house, with the ability to give each its own unique name. Additionally, multiple users can download the app and share information via the cloud. Any users can also be added to the same home and have tasks assigned to them. Notifications and reminders will be utilised on the day the task is due to be completed.

There will also be a list of cleaning tasks predefined such as vacuuming, dusting, clean windows, and so on. Each room will have its own list of tasks depending on the room itself, so your bedroom would have “change sheets, wipe down bedside table, clean windows” and depending on the contents of the bedroom, could also have “clean mirror, wipe down fan, etc”.

Each individual task will also have its own suggested frequency which can be altered at will. Changing sheets could be once a week, vacuuming three times a week, mowing the lawns once every two weeks, take the bins out every Wednesday and put on a load of laundry every two days.

Users can be added and assigned tasks to be completed. So, John could vacuum “Johns room” on Sundays, Wednesdays and Fridays. An alterative assignment process would be by task. So once a week, Sally could clean all windows in the home. There will be multiple ways to view tasks to be done. By timespan will give an overview of everything to be done that day/week/month. By room will give an overview of each room/area and all tasks to be completed within. By user will give an overview of each user and all tasks assigned to them.

Once completed tasks will be checked off until the next time the are due to be completed.

Tools & Technologies

The only hardware needed for this application is a phone or tablet. Software needed would be along the lines of Swift and /or Android Studio for app development, and an emulator in order to test. Microsoft Azure or something similar cloud computing services to make data sync between devices. Photoshop, Sketch or Wireframe.cc for creating wireframes and general design for the app.

Skills Required

Programming language knowledge, such as Python, Java, Swift and/or C++. Decent proficiency using software such as Photoshop, Sketch and Wireframe.cc in order to create the overall layout, graphics and design for the app. Overall the necessary skills should be relatively easy to develop though study, trial and error.

Outcome

The successful use of this app will help maintain order in a household. Whether you’re living alone and just need reminders of what to clean and how often to clean without trying to get it all done in one hit and getting overwhelmed. Or if you share your house with housemates or family you can distribute tasks fairly throughout the household in order to share the load, being sent updates when communal areas have been cleaned or notifications when a task is coming up to be due, or if it is overdue.

This would be particularly impactful for families with teenagers who can spend a lot of time on their devices. If a task isn’t completed on time this would open opportunities for conversational development, where you can detail how much work has been completed by other members of the household compared to someone who isn’t doing their part.
